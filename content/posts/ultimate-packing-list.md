I've lived out of a 40L backpack for seven years. Through trial, error, and many abandoned items, I've refined my packing to just the essentials.

This list works for any climate, any duration, any style of travel.

## The Philosophy

**Less is more.** Every item must justify its weight and space. If you haven't used something in three trips, leave it home.

**Buy there, not here.** You can get shampoo in Thailand. Don't pack 12oz bottles.

**Invest in quality.** A $200 jacket you wear 200 times beats a $50 jacket you replace three times.

## The Bags

**Primary: Osprey Farpoint 40L**
- Perfect carry-on size (fits in overhead bin)
- Opens like a suitcase (easy packing)
- Hideaway straps (converts from backpack to duffel)

**Secondary: 15L daypack**
- For daily adventures while main bag stays at accommodation
- Packable design takes minimal space
- I use Matador Freerain24

## Clothing (7-Day Rotation)

**Tops:**
- 3x merino wool t-shirts (Wool & Prince)
- 1x long-sleeve button-up (wrinkle-free travel shirt)
- 1x lightweight fleece
- 1x packable rain jacket

**Bottoms:**
- 2x travel pants (Western Rise AT Slim Rivet)
- 1x shorts (Patagonia Baggies)
- 1x swim trunks (double as shorts)

**Underwear & Socks:**
- 4x underwear (ExOfficio Give-N-Go)
- 4x socks (Darn Tough merino wool)
- 1x compression socks (for flights)

**Shoes:**
- Everyday: Allbirds Wool Runners (black)
- Backup: Xero minimalist sandals

**Accessories:**
- 1x belt (Grip6, no holes)
- 1x cap (Outdoor Research)
- 1x sunglasses

Total outfits: Infinite combinations. Merino wool = no smell, quick-dry, looks good.

## Tech

**Core:**
- MacBook Air M2 (13")
- iPhone + international SIM
- Kindle Paperwhite
- AirPods Pro
- Portable charger (Anker 20,000mAh)

**Cables & Adapters:**
- USB-C to Lightning
- USB-C to USB-C
- Universal travel adapter (with USB ports)
- Cable organizer pouch

**Optional (depending on work):**
- Portable monitor (ASUS ZenScreen)
- Wireless mouse
- Laptop stand

## Toiletries (Minimal Kit)

Everything fits in a small dopp kit:

- Travel toothbrush + toothpaste
- Deodorant (solid stick, not spray)
- Shampoo bar (lasts 6+ weeks, TSA-friendly)
- Nail clippers
- Razor (safety razor + 10 blades)
- Sunscreen stick
- Lip balm with SPF
- Contact lenses + solution (2-week supply, buy more locally)

**What I don't pack:**
- Full-size bottles of anything
- Towel (hotels have them, hostels rent them)
- Hair dryer (use hotel's or air dry)

## Documents & Money

**Essential:**
- Passport (check expiration date, 6+ months validity)
- Credit cards (2 different networks: Visa + Mastercard)
- Debit card for ATM withdrawals
- Travel insurance details (digital + printed)
- Photocopies of passport (separate from original)

**Money strategy:**
- Carry $200-300 USD cash
- Use credit cards for everything possible (points + fraud protection)
- Withdraw local currency from ATMs as needed

## Health & Wellness

**Medicine:**
- Ibuprofen (pain/inflammation)
- Antihistamine (allergies)
- Imodium (stomach issues)
- Electrolyte packets (LMNT or similar)
- Prescription meds (30-day supply + doctor's note)

**Sleep & Jet Lag:**
- Eye mask (silk, blocks 100% light)
- Earplugs (foam, 33dB reduction)
- Jet lag supplements (Gummly or melatonin)
- Magnesium glycinate

**First Aid:**
- Band-aids
- Antibiotic ointment
- Tweezers
- Small scissors

## Miscellaneous Essentials

**Security:**
- TSA-approved luggage lock
- RFID-blocking wallet
- Pacsafe cable lock (secure bag to furniture)

**Comfort:**
- Reusable water bottle (collapsible or rigid)
- Packing cubes (organize clothing by category)
- Compression bags (for dirty clothes)
- Microfiber quick-dry towel (only if doing hostels/camping)

**Nice to Have:**
- Travel journal + pen
- Deck of cards
- Portable Bluetooth speaker (JBL Clip)

## What Didn't Make the Cut

Items I used to pack but no longer carry:

- **Laptop lock:** Never used it
- **Travel pillow:** Too bulky, hoodie works fine
- **Umbrella:** Rain jacket is enough
- **Guidebooks:** Everything's on phone
- **Multiple shoes:** 2 pairs max
- **Jeans:** Too heavy, slow to dry
- **Cotton anything:** Merino wool or synthetic only

## The Packing System

**Packing cubes organization:**
- Cube 1: Tops
- Cube 2: Bottoms + underwear
- Cube 3: Tech + cables
- Toiletry kit: All hygiene items
- Daypack: Stored inside main bag when not in use

**Pro tip:** Pack heavier items closest to your back. Keeps weight balanced and prevents back pain.

## The Laundry Strategy

With only 7 days of clothes, I do laundry weekly:

- Hand-wash in hotel sink (merino wool dries overnight)
- Use laundromat every 7-10 days ($5-10)
- Emergency: wear something twice (merino doesn't smell)

Never wait for accommodation laundry service. It's overpriced and takes 2-3 days.

## Climate Adaptations

**Cold Weather Add-ons:**
- Lightweight down jacket (packable)
- Thin gloves + beanie
- Thermal base layer (top + bottom)

Everything still fits in 40L.

**Hot Weather Removals:**
- Take out fleece
- Swap pants for more shorts
- Add extra t-shirt

## Budget Breakdown

Building this kit from scratch:

- Backpack: $150-200
- Clothing: $400-600
- Shoes: $150
- Tech: $1,500-2,500 (if buying new)
- Toiletries: $50
- Accessories: $100

**Total: $2,350-3,600**

Expensive upfront, but this gear lasts 3-5+ years. I've spent less on travel gear than friends spend replacing cheap luggage annually.

## The Real Test

Can you live out of this for 30 days? 90 days? A year?

I've done all three. This list works.

You'll refine it based on your personal needs, but start here. Cut ruthlessly. Every pound you remove is freedom.

Travel light. Travel far.
