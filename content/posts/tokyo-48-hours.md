Tokyo is overwhelming in the best possible way. With only 48 hours, you need a strategic plan to experience the city's incredible range without burning out.

This itinerary assumes you're fighting jet lag, so I've built in rest points and balanced intensity with downtime.

## Day 1: Traditional Tokyo

**7:00am - Tsukiji Outer Market**

Start early (helps with jet lag adjustment). Grab fresh sushi breakfast at one of the tiny counter restaurants. The fatty tuna melts in your mouth. Budget: ¥2,000-3,000.

Walk through the market stalls afterward. Skip the tourist omelet place (overpriced and overhyped).

**9:30am - Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa)**

Take the metro to Asakusa. Walk through Nakamise shopping street to Senso-ji, Tokyo's oldest temple. Go early before tour groups arrive.

The incense smoke, massive lantern, and traditional architecture give you immediate culture shock in the best way.

**12:00pm - Lunch in Asakusa**

Try tempura at Daikokuya (¥1,500) or soba noodles at one of the small shops near the temple. Sit at the counter and watch the chefs work.

**2:00pm - TeamLab Borderless (Odaiba)**

Book tickets in advance. This digital art museum is mind-bending: interactive rooms with flowing waterfalls, floating flowers, and infinite mirrors.

Give yourself 90 minutes minimum. Wear comfortable shoes (you'll be walking through dark rooms).

**5:00pm - Hotel Rest Break**

Go back to your hotel for 90 minutes. Shower, rest (no napping!), and recharge. This break prevents Day 1 burnout.

**7:00pm - Shibuya Crossing & Dinner**

Experience the iconic scramble crossing at peak hours. Head up to the Starbucks on the second floor for the aerial view.

Dinner options:
- **Ichiran Ramen** (solo booths, perfect if you're peopled-out)
- **Gyukatsu Motomura** (fried beef cutlet, life-changing)
- **Sushi Zanmai** (late-night sushi, excellent quality)

**9:00pm - Golden Gai (Shinjuku)**

Wander through this neighborhood of tiny bars (most seat 5-8 people). Pick one that looks welcoming and chat with locals. Cover charge: ¥500-1,000.

Don't bar hop aggressively. Find one good spot and settle in.

**11:00pm - Back to Hotel**

Early night to prepare for Day 2. Take your jet lag supplements and aim for 7+ hours of sleep.

## Day 2: Modern Tokyo

**8:00am - Meiji Shrine**

Start in the peaceful forested shrine before the crowds arrive. The giant torii gate and serene atmosphere are the perfect counterbalance to Tokyo's intensity.

Walk through the grounds slowly. If you're lucky, you'll see a traditional wedding procession.

**10:00am - Harajuku & Takeshita Street**

Just outside the shrine, dive into the chaos of Takeshita Street. Teenage fashion, crepes, and overwhelming sensory input.

Don't miss the side streets for quirky vintage shops and cafes.

**12:00pm - Lunch in Omotesando**

Head to the more sophisticated Omotesando area. Try:
- **Afuri Ramen** (yuzu-infused broth)
- **Bills** (Australian brunch, ricotta pancakes)

**2:00pm - Akihabara (Electric Town)**

If you're into anime, gaming, or tech, allocate 2 hours. Visit:
- Multi-story anime shops
- Retro game stores  
- Maid cafes (if you're feeling adventurous)

If it's not your thing, skip to Ginza instead for luxury shopping and architecture.

**5:00pm - Roppongi Hills Observation Deck**

Watch the sunset over Tokyo from the observation deck. On clear days, you can see Mount Fuji.

The city lights coming on as dusk falls is magical.

**7:00pm - Final Dinner**

Splurge on your last night:
- **Sushi Saito** (if you booked months ago and have $400)
- **Narisawa** (innovative Japanese fine dining)
- **Kyubey** (excellent sushi, more accessible)

Or keep it casual with yakitori under the train tracks in Yurakucho.

**9:00pm - Nightcap in Roppongi or Ginza**

End at a rooftop bar like:
- **Two Rooms** (Omotesando)
- **New York Bar** (Park Hyatt, Lost in Translation vibes)

## Practical Tips

**Getting Around:**
- Buy a 72-hour metro pass (¥1,500)
- Google Maps works perfectly for train routing
- Trains stop running around midnight

**Money:**
- Many places are cash-only
- Withdraw from 7-Eleven ATMs

**Jet Lag Strategy:**
- Stay awake until at least 9pm local time
- Get sunlight in the morning  
- Use sleep aids if needed

**Food:**
- Don't tip (it's considered rude)
- Pointing at pictures is acceptable
- Solo dining is common and comfortable

**Emergency Phrases:**
- Sumimasen (excuse me)
- Arigato gozaimasu (thank you)
- Eigo ga hanasemasu ka? (Do you speak English?)

## What I Skipped (And Don't Feel Bad About)

With only 48 hours, I didn't visit:
- Tokyo Skytree (observation deck is fine, not essential)
- Imperial Palace (limited access, better to walk the gardens)
- Roppongi museums (save for a longer trip)

You can't do everything. This itinerary balances iconic experiences with manageable pacing.

## Total Budget Estimate

- Food: ¥10,000-15,000 ($75-110)
- Transportation: ¥2,000 ($15)
- Attractions: ¥5,000 ($35)
- Drinks/nightlife: ¥3,000-5,000 ($20-35)

**Total: $145-195 per day** (excluding accommodation)

Tokyo doesn't have to be expensive. Convenience store food is excellent and cheap. Ramen will fill you up for ¥1,000.

## The Feeling

By hour 47, you'll be exhausted, overstimulated, and already planning your return trip.

Tokyo rewards intensity. Lean into the chaos, embrace the sensory overload, and let the city's energy carry you through the jet lag.
