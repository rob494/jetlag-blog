Travel is incredible for the soul, but it can be brutal on the body. Between disrupted sleep schedules, unfamiliar foods, and hours of sitting, staying healthy on the road takes intentional effort.

As someone who teaches yoga retreats around the world, I've learned to maintain my wellness routine no matter where I am. Here are the ten practices that keep me balanced.

## 1. Morning Movement Ritual (10 Minutes)

Before checking your phone or email, do 10 minutes of gentle movement. My go-to sequence:

- 5 sun salutations
- 2 minutes of hip openers
- 3 minutes of gentle twists

This resets your nervous system and counteracts travel stiffness. No yoga mat needed (hotel carpet works fine).

## 2. Hydration First, Coffee Second

I drink 16oz of room-temperature water before allowing myself coffee. This simple rule ensures I start the day hydrated, which affects energy levels, digestion, and mental clarity.

Add lemon or apple cider vinegar for extra gut health benefits.

## 3. Pack Probiotics

Travel destroys your gut microbiome. Different water, new foods, stress, and time zone changes all disrupt digestion.

I take a daily probiotic (50 billion CFU minimum) starting three days before travel. It's made the difference between enjoying local cuisine and spending half the trip in the bathroom.

## 4. The 20-Minute Walk Rule

Within 2 hours of arriving anywhere new, I take a 20-minute walk. This does multiple things:

- Gets you oriented to the neighborhood  
- Exposes you to natural light (helps with jet lag)
- Counteracts travel stiffness
- Lowers stress hormones

Make it non-negotiable, even if you're exhausted.

## 5. Maintain One Constant

I can't control much while traveling, but I can control my morning routine. No matter where I am, I do:

- 10 minutes of meditation
- Morning movement
- Journaling with coffee

This anchor keeps me grounded when everything else is chaotic.

## 6. The 80/20 Food Rule

I aim for 80% nourishing foods and give myself 20% freedom for local indulgences. This means:

**80% of the time:**
- Vegetables with every meal
- Lean protein
- Staying hydrated
- Avoiding excessive alcohol

**20% of the time:**
- Trying the local specialty (even if it's fried)
- Having that extra glass of wine
- Enjoying dessert

This balance lets me experience the culture without feeling like garbage.

## 7. Set a Screen Curfew

Blue light from phones disrupts melatonin production, making jet lag and sleep issues worse. I set a hard 9pm screen curfew.

After 9pm: reading (paper books), stretching, chatting with locals, or early sleep. No scrolling Instagram in bed.

## 8. Compression Gear for Long Travel Days

On any flight or bus ride over 4 hours, I wear compression socks. They reduce swelling, improve circulation, and help you feel dramatically better upon arrival.

Not glamorous, but functional beats fashionable when you're traveling for wellness.

## 9. Book Accommodations with Movement Space

When choosing hotels or Airbnbs, I specifically look for places with:

- Enough floor space for yoga
- Good natural light
- Quiet mornings

I'll pay an extra $20/night for a space that supports my wellness routine over a cramped, dark room.

## 10. Give Yourself a Recovery Day

For every week of travel, schedule one full recovery day with nothing on the agenda. Sleep in, move gently, hydrate, eat well, and do nothing productive.

This rest day prevents burnout and lets your nervous system recalibrate.

## The Bigger Picture

Wellness while traveling isn't about perfection. It's about maintaining enough healthy habits that you can enjoy the adventure without running yourself into the ground.

Start with two or three of these practices and build from there. Your body (and your travel experiences) will thank you.
